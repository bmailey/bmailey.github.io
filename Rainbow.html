Rainbow trout, as seen pictured above, are known for their vibrant coloration. They usually have a silver body with pink or red stripes running along their bodies. They are referred to as "rainbow" trout because of these colorings. Sometimes rainbow trout’s fins can turn light orange or light red depending on the season. Montana rainbow trout can grow between 14 and 30 inches and can weigh more than 10 pounds

Currently, the recorded Montana record for a rainbow trout is 38.62 inches. The world record rainbow trout was caught in Lake Diefenbaker, Saskatchewan, Canada. The trout was caught by Sean Konrad and was 48 pounds. The length of the fish was 42 inches and is was 32 inches wide.
